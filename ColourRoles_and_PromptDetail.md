# Track A — Colour Role Assignment & Detailed Prompt Templates

## Colour Harmony Rule (applies to ALL Track A palettes)

**Core rule:** The background colour stays vivid and lively. BUT the illustration subject, frame/motif, and text MUST use contrasting palette colours — never the same hue as the background. This creates visual hierarchy: vivid background → distinct frame → standout illustration → readable text.

**Prompt injection pattern:**
"Background filled with [canvas_fill]. Decorative frame and motifs rendered in [frame_colour], clearly distinct from background. Illustration subject rendered primarily in [illus_primary] with [illus_accent] details — the subject must NOT be rendered in [canvas_fill] tones, it must visually pop against the background. Reserve space for text in [text_colour]."

## Colour Harmony Enforcement Rules

1. **Never render illustration in the dominant background colour.** If BG is orange, illustration must use maroon/cream/gold/red — never orange.
2. **Frame/motif colour must be visually distinct from background.** Gold frame on most backgrounds works. On gold/saffron backgrounds, use maroon or deep brown frame.
3. **Text colour must pass 4.5:1 contrast ratio** against the background. Maroon on orange, white on dark backgrounds, gold on blue/purple/teal.
4. **Sparkle particles are always white (#FFFFFF)** regardless of palette — they need to pop.
5. **The negative prompt must include the background colour as a banned colour for the illustration.** E.g., "--no orange chai cup" when background is orange.
